Part 1.1: Current employability

Q: What are you wanting to achieve through this process, what key areas are you going to focus on for your employability? Do you have any gaps in your professional skills that you would like to build upon, are you ready for employment of further post graduate opportunities?

As I embark upon the Professional Practice Employability Profile, I have the opportunity to tailor the 12 (or more!) activities I’ll be doing to ensure I get the most out of this assignment as possible. I’ll aim for a balance; I wish to incorporate some activities I already do which are relevant to my employability, but I also want to seek out some new opportunities that I haven’t engaged with before. I’m in a position where I’m not sure what I’ll be doing after graduation, so I will be seeking a wide range of activities to broaden my perspective.

For starters, I’ll focus on some activities I’m already doing – such as my involvement in Scouts and Makerspace. Perhaps even some recent adventures, like Flinders’ game jam. Beyond that, the key areas I wish to focus on include connecting with industry. I’d like to be able to talk to people who work at companies I’m interested in and see what their lives are like. If I can arrange some kind of 1-on-1 coffee catch up that would be even better! Otherwise, going to an industry night or event run by someone in the computer-science sector would work too. Of course, this will mean I can form solid connections before I make my own journey into post-uni life.

Another area I’d like to focus on is broadening my network within Uni. I’ve spent 2 years intending to go to AUMS & CS Club events, and I think this assignment is the perfect incentive to actually go and connect with my peers. This has the obvious benefit that many of these peers will be going into the workforce with me, and builds my soft interpersonal skills.

Generally, I find it difficult to describe my own professional skills. That said, one thing I think I excel at is leadership & collaboration (for example: through my experience in scouting). I also like to think I’m good at communicating ideas really effectively, particularly when it comes to disseminating difficult or technical concepts to peers or an audience. Having said that, I’m not perfect (yes, it’s true). One area I desperately need to work on is self-promotion. I’ve never been great at talking about myself (even writing this is very difficult!), so hopefully attending some employability or resume-writing activities could help with that. Seeking feedback when appropriate will be a good way to gauge my progress.

Also, whilst I can be good at communicating where I’m comfortable, I need to develop my ability to network in unfamiliar environments. I am often a bit nervous at first. By throwing myself into new activities (and listening during professional practice workshops!) I will try to get better at making this first connection.

At this current stage, I would have to admit I’m not quite ready for employment/ post-graduation! I think this will, in part, change as the time nears and I build up to it psychologically. But also, if I try to target the areas I’ve discussed above, the employability profile will be invaluable in preparing me for next year. I look forward to seeing how it goes!
