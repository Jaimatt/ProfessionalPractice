Professional Practice incentivising me to try something new seems to be a theme across several of these activities. For many, I quite enjoyed them & the AllUni Programming Competition was no exception. This was an all-day international event run by universities across Australia and New Zealand. 
I had never done serious competitive programming before, but nonetheless decided to throw myself in the deep end (and hopefully not drown). I convinced some friends to drown with me, and we enrolled in the advanced division. On the day, we found some of the puzzles to be very difficult, and perhaps a bit beyond us! But other puzzles were manageable and we were able to get some points from them. Frustratingly, for a couple we wrote working solutions, but didn’t receive credit as our code deigned to take more than a second to run.
After four hours of stressful coding, we finished 56th. Hardly worthy of a trophy, but for a first effort I’m very proud!
This competition was a good opportunity to practice programming in a collaborative team environment, work through problems together, and network with peers. These sorts of events can also be a way to get the attention of sponsoring companies, which I’ll keep in mind for next time I participate in one.
