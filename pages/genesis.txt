When I started this game jam, was my intention to use it for Professional Practice? Perhaps not. But rather fortuitously I managed to kill two birds with one stone, as it were. And having begun the game jam on 16th February, it just snuck into the acceptable date range!
For the uninitiated, a “game jam” is an event where you’re given a theme – and a deadline – and need to create an entire video game to fit the theme. This particular event was run by the Flinders University Games Research Lab. My team consisted of myself and 3 others who study game development at Flinders.
The theme we were given was “Day One”. As a team, we spoke about it for several hours and decided on a biblical tone, where the player (God) is tasked with introducing light into the world through a series of puzzles of increasing complexity. Fittingly, we dubbed the game “genesis”.
Over the four days of the competition, most of my waking hours were dedicated to the game. I had been tasked with most of the programming, particularly surrounding the implementation of the various game “tiles” and their underlying logic. Participating taught me how to work to a <i>tight</i> deadline, necessitated intense & very close collaboration, build up my technical skills (particularly in Unity, and C#), and required me to set goals and, at times, how to adjust those goals while still delivering a satisfactory product.
There were 10 total submissions to the competition – many very good – and we won the audience choice award! You’re welcome to play it yourself with the link above.
