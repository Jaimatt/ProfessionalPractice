If one of these professional practice activities directly led to an opportunity for me in the near future, that would be very welcome. So, when the option was presented to me, I thought it wise to attend an Internship Information Session run by Penny de Byl, my current program director. This online session focussed on how to apply for internships relating to computer science, where you should be looking, and important details about recording internships with the university.
During this session, we covered different kinds of internships (which differ predominantly in their length) and the university’s approach to each. For instance, longer internships cannot be undertaken after your capstone course – which would more or less preclude me from doing one! And elective internships function a bit differently, as these don’t come with course credit. We went over some example student cases, and spoke about places to look for internships.
This was all useful information I’ll need to keep in mind when planning what the next year or so of my studies will look like.
