Fitting snugly into the category of ‘<i>things I would have done anyway, but it’s nice to get assignment credit for</i>’ is Advent of Code 2023. Advent of Code is an annual series of festive programming puzzles that release throughout each December. I first participated in 2025 at request of a friend. We both did the puzzles each day, we competed, I won, I barely think about it though.
After enjoying it so much in December, I decided to backdate my progress and do some earlier years’ puzzles. In my spare time last summer, I gradually worked on the 2024 puzzles. And over the course of this semester, I’ve worked on some of the 2023 puzzles.
I’ve found that these puzzles require me to directly implement things I learn about during my degree. For instance, they don’t tell you how to go about it, but I’ve had to use both of Dijkstra’s and Kruskal’s algorithms for some puzzles. Some puzzles are really hard algorithmically and require complex, sophisticated solutions. I’ve had some solutions work too slowly and have to implement time-saving techniques I’ve studied like memoisation to improve algorithmic performance. 
I still haven’t solved <i>every</i> puzzle from 2023; some are just a bit beyond me! Nonetheless, being able to practically implement concepts from my studies into complex problems refines my technical algorithmic skills. AoC is a very famous puzzle; my involvement in it and documentation of my work can be an enticing entry on my professional portfolio.
