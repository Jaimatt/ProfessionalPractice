I think I’d be doing this class a disservice if I managed to get through it without experiencing at least one of the CareerHub’s famous series of online ‘career and employability’ seminars. Of their options, I chosen to join one called “Finding Casual Work”.
Turns out, not quite as boring as I thought it would be! Instead, it was informative and brought up ideas that would not have crossed my mind otherwise. The seminar discussed topics like a “job search strategy”, involving looking at market trends, and considering your goals. They spoke about “hidden jobs”, likening the job market to an iceberg. They also discussed workplace laws, networking, elevator pitches, and many other things that I couldn’t summarise in these 200 words!
I’m at a stage where I’m still a semester away from graduating. In the intervening time, I would like to do some casual work. It could be vaguely related to my field, or otherwise. In any case, it would be a good way to gain some professional experience beyond the volunteering I do. Some of this experience may be able to transfer to my work post-graduation. After this seminar, I feel far more equipped to pursue this.
