I’ve spent my degree contemplating the possibility of abandoning computer science and disappearing entirely into the rabbit warren that is Pure Mathematics. For a time, I wished to major in both (but the university has other ideas). Nonetheless, it’s certainly a topic I wish to learn about and remains an option for post-graduate study. To better explore this option, I decided to attend “The Other Pure Seminar”. 
TOPS is a series of weekly seminars run by post-graduate pure students & I attended one of the last ones of the semester. Now, I’m pretty sure most of it was in English. There were certainly words I recognised like “the”, “and”, or perhaps even “therefore”. Regrettably, everything between remains a mystery! The seminar gave a problem setup, then aimed to determine whether there was a metric g̃ conformal to g such that g̃ has constant scalar curvature. Your guess as to what that means is as good as mine…
Much as I struggled with the content, I do think this was a valuable experience. I’m not intimidated by the mathematics itself – I just hadn’t studied it. But if I am to pursue Pure, this is the sort of environment I should expect to find myself, so it’s good to see what it’s like. It was also nice to meet some of the people I may be working with were I to go down this pathway.
