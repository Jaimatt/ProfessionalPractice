Makerspace Adelaide is one of those places where it’s remarkably easy to turn up for an hour and somehow still be there at closing time. As a community-run not-for-profit, Makerspace aims to make technology, engineering, and creative pursuits more accessible by offering a wide variety of tools and equipment to its members. I first started volunteering there at the start of 2024 and have since spent many weekends helping run the space, providing guidance to members, working on a few of my own projects, and building a network of technology-focussed makers.
I’ve included it as an “activity” here not because of a great leap in my relationship with Makerspace, but rather because of my continued involvement. In the past six months members have worked on a tessellation exhibit, many intricate costumes, a life-size star-wars figure, a banana shaped ruler (don’t ask), a model train, a rotary device for a hologram machine, and so much more! Makerspace has also welcomed an exciting new type of laser cutter, which we’re in the process of learning all the tricks for.
My involvement at Makerspace also extends to running our fortnightly laser cutter induction sessions. These teach newer members how to safely operate our laser cutters from design to finished product, and ideally not lose our focussing tools & lenses (it reflects badly on me if they do!).
Volunteering at Makerspace has been very rewarding, and I’m sure I will continue to do it for years to come.
