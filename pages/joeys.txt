Despite being a computer science student, I do occasionally touch grass. In fact, I’ve been involved in scouting for most of my life. Towards the end of high school, I took on a role as a leader for Joey scouts (youth aged 5-7). In the 3 years since then, I haven’t been kicked out just yet, so I must be doing something right.
This past semester has seen a drastic rise in our joey population. We’re up to 18 now, which can become a little unruly at times! But with the help of the other Joey leaders present we manage. In our weekly sessions, we try to create fun, engaging activities that also trick the kids into learning important outdoor skills. If we do a climbing night, the joeys learn how to use a harness and carabiner. On a hiking night, we can cover basic first aid. Or on a cooking night we can go over starting a simple fire.
My own ability to work with children has drastically improved, and it’s something I now find very rewarding. Predictably, I’ve been able to develop my own leadership skills whilst also trying to encourage our youth to take on small leadership roles themselves. And scouts always a great opportunity to meet people through a shared interest – for instance this semester I found out one of the Joeys’ dads works at the university as an academic within Machine Learning!
The Joeys never cease to amaze me. In April we went camping outdoors and everyone was super resilient. They set up their own tents, cooked their dinner. Though the semester’s over, I will of course be continuing my involvement with Joeys.
