I went into this assignment hoping to participate in a broad range of activities. In aid of this, I attended a session that focussed on a specific industry, namely education (primary and secondary). This inequity workshop was run by Teach for Australia, and discussed educational inequity within South Australia and Australia.
There are many factors, I learned, that contribute to educational inequity. Money, infrastructure, resources, local ethnicities. But the factor that this session focussed on most was <i>location</i>. The location of schools directly influences every other factor, compounding the problem dramatically. TFA aims to counter this under-investment in Australia’s rural education by placing skilled graduates in rural institutions whilst they undergo a Master of Teaching online.
This session had a particular focus on STEM graduates. According to TFA, a huge proportion of STEM teachers in rural areas do not have the ideal qualifications for their field, which leads to sub-par education and difficulties for students to engage with & pursue STEM industries.
STEM education is a topic of huge personal importance to me, and it saddens me that so many students aren’t receiving the same opportunities I have been lucky to receive in my education. I’m not sure if teaching is the career pathway for me, but after this session it certainly remains an option.
