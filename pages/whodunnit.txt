The careers hub delivered a somewhat unusual activity in the form of this “whodunnit”. I like computer science, and I like a good mystery, so I enrolled the moment I saw it! And as a bonus, I managed to drag a couple of reluctant friends along too.
What proceeded was not what I was expecting, but was very interesting. Cyber security is not my specialisation, so I don’t have much experience in this domain. This workshop gave us a list of “suspects” – people who were associated with a breach – and asked us to deduce the guilty party. For each “suspect” we were provided with motives, positions, and their own access to sensitive systems. We were given a log of activities within the system, news articles reporting on the breach, and various other documents. From these we had to draw connections and determine who did it.
We were wrong.
But that’s okay! Truthfully, none of us had cyber security experience so we didn’t have much to draw on. For what it’s worth, we <i>did</i> win the quiz afterwards (yes, I do need to brag about that).
This activity was a great way to break into an area with which I don’t have much familiarity, get some experience, and work collaboratively in a team to solve a complex problem (or not solve it, I guess).
