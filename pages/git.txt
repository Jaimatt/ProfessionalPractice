Volunteering at Makerspace Adelaide has a habit of bringing about interesting events & opportunities. Being amongst the most reputable & accessible public workshops, we’re the first contact for many events, exhibits, & functions related to technology and our volunteers will generally help run these. This manifested for me in the form of St. Peters Girls’ inter-school Girls-in-tech Expo.
Alongside other workshops, this school wanted to run a laser-cutting class that introduced the technology to the students & allowed them to actively engage with it. I was tasked with being the primary contact person for Melissa (the event coordinator at St Peters), working out exactly what was needed of us, designing the workshop, and facilitating it on the day. 
In the end we had three groups of about 15 students each. We ran them through the theory behind laser cutting, had a look-and-touch session with a myriad of sample projects we constructed, and each student got to watch the cutting of and take home a personalised keychain!
This was an exciting event that gave me some more experience both organising behind-the-scenes, and working with students on the day. It was a joy to see how much the students loved seeing the laser cutter in action. Hopefully through events like this, the girls we spoke to will feel empowered to later pursue careers in technology.
