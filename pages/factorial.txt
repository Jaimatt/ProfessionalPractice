100 factorial is really just a big number. It’s defined as 100 × 99 × 98 × 97 × … × 2 × 1 and is around 9.3 ×10<sup>157</sup>. As it happens, 100 factorial is <i>also</i> the name of the Math Learning Centre’s weekly art/puzzle/game sessions in the Hub. Throughout this semester, I was a regular at these Wednesday sessions during which I got the opportunity to engage with other students, and explore mathematics in interesting ways.
The focus of these sessions tends to be on recreational mathematics, with foundational logic & pure mathematics being the primary underlying principles. In some cases, we’ll explore the mathematics behind the puzzles & games, which is always fascinating. As someone who has spent a lot of time studying pure mathematics, and has considered majoring in the topic, these sessions have been interesting to engage with academically. And beyond the mathematical rigor, the sessions have shown me a fun & relaxing with to engage with the topic.
Additionally, the AU staff member who facilitates them, David Butler, is in the field of Pure Math so it’s been useful being able to network with him through these informal sessions.
